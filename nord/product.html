THE STORY OF THIS COFFEE

Konga washing station, Yirgacheffe.

Konga sits in the heart of Yirgacheffe, where hundreds of smallholders deliver ripe cherry to a single washing station. The coffee is depulped, fermented, and washed clean — a process that strips away everything but the clarity of the fruit.

We have bought from this station for three seasons now. The relationship matters: it means consistency from the same growers, and a price that rewards the care they put into selective picking.

This lot is roasted light. We want the jasmine and bergamot the altitude gives it — not the flavours a darker roast would impose.
